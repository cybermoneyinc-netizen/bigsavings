15 Easy Ways to Lower Your Energy Bills This Month

8 min read

Energy costs keep rising, but there are plenty of simple ways to fight back. These tips require little to no upfront investment and can start saving you money immediately.

Heating and Cooling

1. Adjust Your Thermostat: Lower your thermostat by 1-2 degrees in winter and raise it in summer. You won't notice the difference, but your bill will show it.

2. Use a Programmable Thermostat: Set it to reduce heating/cooling when you're asleep or away. Smart thermostats can save up to 15% on heating and cooling costs.

3. Seal Air Leaks: Check windows and doors for drafts. Weatherstripping and caulk are inexpensive fixes that make a big difference.

4. Use Ceiling Fans Wisely: Run fans counterclockwise in summer to create a cooling breeze. In winter, reverse them to push warm air down.

Lighting

5. Switch to LED Bulbs: LEDs use 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs and last 25 times longer. They pay for themselves quickly.

6. Use Natural Light: Open curtains during the day instead of turning on lights. Position desks and seating areas near windows.

7. Turn Off Lights: Make it a habit to turn off lights when leaving a room. Consider motion-sensor switches for forgetful family members.

Appliances

8. Unplug Devices: Electronics draw power even when turned off (phantom load). Unplug chargers, TVs, and other devices when not in use, or use smart power strips.

9. Run Full Loads: Only run your dishwasher and washing machine with full loads. This maximizes efficiency per cycle.

10. Air Dry When Possible: Skip the heated dry cycle on your dishwasher. Hang clothes to dry instead of using the dryer.

11. Clean Your Refrigerator Coils: Dusty coils make your fridge work harder. Clean them every six months for optimal efficiency.

Water Heating

12. Lower Water Heater Temperature: Set it to 120¬∞F instead of the default 140¬∞F. You'll save energy without sacrificing comfort.

13. Take Shorter Showers: Cutting your shower by just 2 minutes can save significant water and energy costs over a year.

14. Fix Leaky Faucets: A dripping hot water faucet wastes both water and energy. Fix leaks promptly.

General Tips

15. Get an Energy Audit: Many utility companies offer free home energy audits. They'll identify the biggest opportunities for savings in your specific home.

Track Your Progress

Compare your bills month-over-month and year-over-year to see the impact of your changes. Many utility companies offer online tools to track usage patterns and identify opportunities for additional savings.
